AR AI Airways: Intubation Training Solution for Rural Vermont Doctors

See more

AR AI Airways

Many paramedics, doctors, and healthcare professionals do not come across difficult intubation scenarios (ex: bloody or obstructed) when receiving airway maintenance training. While these individuals do learn the techniques for a standard intubation, this training often falls short when they are come across a non-standard, difficult airway scenarios and more stressful environments.

We evaluated this issue from the standpoint of three stakeholders: Emergency Medicine Practitioners and Emergency Medical Researchers.

The final solution was a high-fidelity, easy-to-transport difficult airway augmented reality intubation simulation that will be compatible with standard manikins and can be used in areas that vary in resources. We were able to bring a prototype to the conference of the Society of American Emergency Medicine and will be user testing with paramedics!

  • We integrated sensor and laryngoscope live video feed into the Metaquest experience.

  • Implemented a dependency graph for gameplay and performance scoring.

  • Witt API integration to evaluate voice commands from users via virtual "nurse".

  • Tools: Raspberry Pi, Bash, Unity, Metaquest

Art and AI

New advancements in generative AI art have sparked conversations about the substitution of artists and designers. Yet, this narrow discourse ignores the large range of artists that employ AI and are growing to employ AI as a critical part of their artistic repertoire. I proposed a project that sought to survey the emerging engagement between artists and AI in how they currently use these technologies and the breadth of how artists do so. The eventual goal in this project is to utilize AI in my final seminar as a Studio Art major and to synthesize this de-facto user-experience research into a thesis as a Computer Science major. Throughout the timeline of this project, AI will be viewed in four main ways: co-creator, subject, practice, or tool over the course of this investigation.

My existing efforts to accomplish these goals, I have interviewed 10+ current practicing artists that use AI in their work including Whitney featured artist Maya Man and Software Studies scholar Winnie Soon. Moreover, I have taken courses like Artificial Intelligence, Human Computer Interaction, and Audio Visual Installation to support these efforts.

  • Awarded $20K in funds to pursue research interests.

  • Conducted a lengthy literature review of field, critically evaluating literature on the ethics of AI, defining creativity, media arts, and software studies.

  • Traveled to exhibitions in Los Angeles and Hong Kong to see pieces in real life.

COSC76: Minimax ChessBot

See more

Minimax ChessBot

Developed and implemented Minimax and Alpha-Beta pruning algorithms for game-playing AI using recursive approaches. Both implementations shared a common architecture that included depth control for iterative deepening, node visit tracking for performance analysis, and toggleable versions to switch between iterative and non-iterative execution modes.

Integrated a transposition table as a hash-based caching system to optimize performance by storing and retrieving previously evaluated game states, eliminating redundant computations. The core Minimax algorithm checked the transposition table before evaluating positions, implemented cutoff tests based on depth limits and game-over conditions, and recursively scored legal moves to select optimal plays for both maximizing and minimizing players.

Extended the Minimax implementation to Alpha-Beta pruning by incorporating alpha and beta parameters for branch pruning, significantly improving search efficiency. Designed and tested multiple heuristic evaluation functions, including a Null Move Heuristic, to experiment with different AI decision-making strategies and compare their effectiveness in gameplay scenarios.

  • Successfully implemented Minimax algorithm with Alpha Beta pruning.

  • Created multiple different heuristics for the chess program including a capture target heuristics.

  • Evaluated the performance of the algorithm at different depths, including with implemented iterative deepening.

  • Tools: Python, Python-Chess, Minimax, Alpha Beta Pruning, Good Old Fashioned AI
